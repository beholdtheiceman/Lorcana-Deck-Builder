## Detailed Matchup Breakdowns — From Amber Ruby Toys Perspective

### Amber/Ruby Toys vs. Dogs (Amber/Emerald)

**Assessment:** Even — Competitive but Dogs is rated #1 deck; requires execution

**Game Plan:**
Two approaches — either banish their singers before they can sing Under the Sea, or go bigger so Under the Sea has less impact.

**Key Threats to Answer:**
- Their singers — prevent Under the Sea from generating value
- Chromicon: Don't fear it. Most Toys characters have "when played" abilities, and those that don't trigger Babyhead/Jesse. Sequence plays correctly around Chromicon and its impact is neutralized.

**Key Cards (for you):** Headless Horseman, Sid Phillips, Jesse Woodard, Babyhead

**Win Line:** Disruption path. Use Horseman and Sid while leaning on larger characters. They have to turn their characters sideways to win — that's when Jesse/Babyhead triggers win combat.

**Mulligan for this matchup:** Prioritize Hamm (enables early Horseman) over normal priority list. Headless Horseman at 4 copies for a reason — find it.

**Note on Toys variant of Dogs:** Sid Phillips is the most important card in the toys mirror. He hits OPPONENT'S toys too — opponents' own Sids will also hit yours. Sequence around this.

### Amber/Ruby Toys vs. Blurple (Amethyst/Sapphire)

**Assessment:** Even — One of the more winnable non-favorable matchups if approached correctly

**Key Threats:**
- Merryweather (4/5 Ward) — use Candy Drift
- Dumbo (Evasive) — save Horseman to clear after Jesse trigger (unless they're playing Genie, in which case play around it)
- Demona — the critical card; her impact collapses if she can't banish challengers
- Merryweather → Elsa → Demona → Hades is their "nut draw" — play to prevent this sequence

**Game Plan:**
Force them into action. Make sacrifices you wouldn't normally make to pivot into a favorable board position.

- Quest with Woody (5) proactively even into his potential death — force them to challenge early, reducing their Demona targets
- Dump your hand BEFORE their Demona turn — rebuild quickly after her effect
- Their removal ramps you (skip inking low-cost toys, just play them to the board)
- Bodies win this matchup — flood the board and let them make decisions under pressure

**Critical insight:** Their removal (Merryweather, Tipo challenges) ramps you for ink. Don't ink cards you can just play for free. Keep pressure up constantly.

**Mulligan:** Keep Candy Drift as backup for Merryweather. Bullseye is your strongest non-boss monster here. Babyhead is crucial — keeps cards flowing when they challenge your characters.

### Amber/Ruby Toys vs. Amber/Steel

**Assessment:** Even — Both decks can grind; execution-dependent

**Key Threats:**
- Akood (enables their early large threats)
- Strength of a Raging Fire — targeted disruption reduces its impact
- Angel (recurring damage) — maintain 2-willpower characters to dodge her damage

**Game Plan:** Disruption path.

- Maintain 2-willpower characters on board — Angel's damage threshold is key
- Disrupt their singers to reduce Strength of a Raging Fire impact
- Woody (5) gives all your toys +1 willpower (stacks for multiple Woodys) — important buffer
- Candy Drift removes their large characters if they make greedy quests or challenges
- Use Horseman and Sid to navigate into the mid-game

**Mulligan:** Headless Horseman is your primary 2-willpower-maintaining piece. Woody (1) on turn 1 is standard. Horseman + Woody + Hamm is ideal.

**Note:** Toys variant of Amber Steel exists. Amber Ruby is favored against that variant — Sid is the most important card in the toys mirrors.

### Amber/Ruby Toys vs. YP Dale (Amethyst/Amber)

**Assessment:** Unfavored (second worst matchup) — Requires Horseman × 4

**Key Threats:**
- Dale (must be answered — deck is significantly weaker without him)
- Elsa (challenges for 5 with Dale on board — banishes almost anything)
- Genie (resource generation)
- Cheshire Cat (damage redirection)
- NOTE: Jesse triggers don't fire with Dale on board — this is critical

**Game Plan:**
- Find multiple Headless Horsemen — he is in the list at 4 copies specifically for this matchup
- Removal priority: Dale first > Genie/Elsa/Cat (board-state dependent; their deck is much weaker without Dale)
- Horseman → Horseman → Sid is the standard win line
- Bullseye is one of your best cards here — early body and efficient challenger
- Build your normal board and continually disrupt them

**Mulligan:** Prioritize Headless Horseman heavily. Normal opener still valid but shift 2-drop priority toward Hamm (enables early Horseman).

**Key removal hierarchy vs. YP Dale:**
1. Dale (shuts off all their buffs)
2. Genie (stops resource generation)
3. Elsa (removes their main challenger)
4. Cheshire Cat (stops damage redirection)
_Adjust based on what's immediately threatening your game plan_

**Reality check:** This is one of your two worst matchups. If cards don't fall your way, that's okay.

### Amber/Ruby Toys vs. Sapphire/Steel

**Assessment:** Favored — Pressure path wins

**Key Threats:**
- Goliath (card draw engine — DO NOT let them keep this)
- Tinker Bell (dangerous challenger — don't let them attack freely)
- The "unlock" — once they reach high ink, their deck becomes overwhelming

**Game Plan:** Pressure path. Race to 20.

- Woody → Bullseye → Shift Woody, free character = Hamm; play on same turn
- Sid is the primary removal tool here (their character lineup is beefy — Horseman harder to use)
- Remove Goliath immediately — puts them on drawing 1 card per turn; their deck crumbles
- Do NOT draw when Goliath is active — it benefits them more than you (their deck built around it)
- Race aggressively; if the game goes late, they have the advantage

**Mulligan:** Standard opener. Hamm prioritized as free off Woody Shift.

### Amber/Ruby Toys vs. Amethyst/Steel

**Assessment:** Even — Disruption path works

**Key Threats:**
- Yzma (must be removed immediately — card advantage engine)
- Cheshire Cat (damage redirection)

**Game Plan:** Disruption path.

- Remove Yzma ASAP — answer her and the rest is "smooth sailing"
- Use Jesse and Babyhead triggers to win combat (small toys beat large characters)
- Mid-game: Use Sid as lore burst — trade 1-lore characters (they get banished, each triggers Sid for 2 lore)
- Candy Drift removes large characters and expands your combat options
- Go wide to spread their decision points across multiple threats

**Dwarves variant:** Less interaction than traditional — easier to navigate. Same general plan.

### Amber/Ruby Toys vs. Ruby/Amethyst

**Assessment:** Favored

**Key Threats:**
- Elisa Meza (shuts off Jesse/Della triggers — removes portion of your effectiveness)

**Game Plan:**
- Go wider than them; apply pressure
- Force them to challenge — their most problematic cards (Elisa) are their best challengers; use this against them
- When they challenge: remove their threat AND re-enable your synergies
- Higher density of explosive plays than Ruby/Amethyst; free toys (Hand, Frog, Ducky) mean your board rebuilds faster
- Apply same principles as vs. Blurple — make them act, then capitalize on their action

**Mulligan:** Standard Woody into Bullseye opener preferred. Normal curving is correct here (they're not ramping).

### Amber/Ruby Toys vs. Emerald/Sapphire Control

**Assessment:** Unfavored (worst matchup) — But winnable with correct approach

**Key Threats:**
- Mass Magical Scouring (MMS) — board wipe; must be planned around
- Prince Phillip (shifted) — unbeatable threat once resolved
- Their entire game plan — they want to go long; you cannot let that happen

**Game Plan:** Maximum aggression.

- Play as aggressive as possible — spread their resources before they set up
- Force them to use MMS on early threats instead of saving it for the Prince Phillip combo
- Alternate alter path: prioritize early aggression cards you'd normally skip
- Goal: reach 16+ lore before their board wipe
  - At 16+ lore, hold Hand/Ptera + Sid for explosive follow-up AFTER their board wipe
  - Sid triggers after the wipe can steal the last 4 lore
- If you can't end before their late game, hold pieces to flood the board and force a SECOND board wipe

**Mulligan:** Accept sub-optimal hands you'd normally alter away — getting on board fast is paramount. Early aggression cards take higher priority than your normal nut draw.

## Cross-Archetype Matchup Principles (Set 12)

### When you are the Beatdown (vs. control/slower decks):
- Quest every possible turn — lore math is ruthless
- Horseman + Sid disruption is your "control" tool even as the beatdown
- Free Ruby toys maintain board presence after disruption — use them to flood after exchanges
- DO NOT miss lethal chasing more value

### When you are the Control (vs. faster decks):
- Wide boards spread opponent's decision points — force bad trades
- Babyhead triggers upgrade 1-cost toys into threats; Jesse triggers protect them
- Candy Drift lets weak toys trade into any target — use offensively, not just defensively
- Dump your hand before their key turns (Demona, Be Prepared, etc.)

### Universal Rules (Set 12 Meta):
- ALWAYS draw first (Jesse, Woody, songs) before committing any ink
- Chromicon: Sequence correctly and its impact is neutral-to-positive for you
- Sid Phillips: Clear the board first so opponent has fewer "choice" options; in mirrors, he hits THEIR toys too
- 2-willpower characters: Key survival stat vs. Angel (Amber Steel) and recurring damage effects
- Burn lore (Giant Cobra, Dash Parr): These bypass board state — if opponent is running them, must race lore even harder
