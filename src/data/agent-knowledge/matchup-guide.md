# Lorcana Matchup Guide
_General matchup dynamics between major archetypes_

Use this file when a user asks: "how do I play against X?", "what's the gameplan vs Y?", "is my deck good against the meta?", or when reviewing deck construction for a specific tournament field.

---

## How to Read This Guide

Each matchup is written from the perspective of the **row deck** facing the **column deck**.
- **Favored** = this deck wins more than 55% of games in ideal circumstances
- **Even** = roughly 50/50, small edges possible with play
- **Unfavored** = this deck loses more than 55% of games

Then specific guidance follows for each major pairing.

## Detailed Matchup Breakdowns

---

### Ruby/Sapphire Midrange vs. Amethyst/Steel Control

**Ruby/Sapphire perspective: Slightly Favored**

The tempo and card draw of Ruby/Sapphire lets it stay ahead on lore while managing the most threatening Steel characters. The key is not overextending into Be Prepared range.

**Game Plan (Ruby/Sapphire):**
- Apply early pressure with 2–3 cost characters but keep hand size healthy
- Do NOT put 3+ characters on board when opponent has 7+ Amethyst ink open — Be Prepared blowout risk
- Keep Fire the Cannons! and Dragon Fire for Steel Bodyguard characters that are blocking your win
- Use Sapphire draw to replace the resources lost to bounce
- Win by staying 3–5 lore ahead and closing before Amethyst/Steel's big threats stabilize

**Key Threats to Answer:**
- Yen Sid - Powerful Sorcerer: Must be challenged with a high-strength character or removed; if it quests once it's a 4-lore swing
- Maleficent - Monstrous Dragon: If this resolves with a board state, you lose. Apply enough pressure that they can't wait to cast it
- Be Prepared: Play around it by keeping 2 characters or fewer on board; sacrifice the weakest one if forced to overextend

**Sideboard / Tech:** Ward characters to survive targeted bounce; redundant threats so bounce never fully resets your gameplan

---

### Ruby/Sapphire Midrange vs. Emerald/Ruby Aggro

**Ruby/Sapphire perspective: Slightly Favored**

Ruby/Sapphire's removal suite cleanly answers Emerald/Ruby's Evasive threats, and its 3–4 cost characters are sturdier than aggro's board. The danger is a fast start where Evasive characters quest before removal is online.

**Game Plan (Ruby/Sapphire):**
- Prioritize using Fire the Cannons! and Dragon Fire on Evasive characters — they're the real lore generators
- Use Sapphire draw to ensure removal doesn't run out
- Don't trade characters if you can banish their Evasive threat with a spell instead
- Once their Evasive characters are gone, your 3–4 cost bodies dominate the board

**Key Threats to Answer:**
- Peter Pan - Never Landing: Evasive + 3 lore; must be handled by a spell or another Evasive character; top priority
- Tinker Bell - Giant Fairy: Cheap, high-lore Evasive; Fire the Cannons! is the correct answer
- Simba - Protective Cub: Low-cost Evasive starter; kill on sight before it snowballs

---

### Amethyst/Steel Control vs. Emerald/Ruby Aggro

**Amethyst/Steel perspective: Unfavored**

This is the hardest matchup for Amethyst/Steel. The aggro deck races under bounce and generates lore so fast that control can't keep up. Be Prepared is the key swing card.

**Game Plan (Amethyst/Steel):**
- Survive to turn 7 by any means necessary; that's when Be Prepared online
- Use Steel Bodyguard characters aggressively to block questing paths
- Bounce the single highest-lore character each turn — target Peter Pan, not the swarm
- Win by casting Be Prepared when they have 3+ small characters and following up with a big Steel threat

**If Playing Emerald/Ruby vs. This Matchup:**
- Flood the board wide but play around Be Prepared (don't commit all characters at once)
- Prioritize Evasive characters — they keep questing even if the rest of the board is bounced
- Win before turn 7 or this gets very hard

---

### Emerald/Ruby Aggro vs. Amber/Sapphire Ramp

**Emerald/Ruby perspective: Favored**

Ramp decks are slow to develop and have minimal early interaction. Aggro's Evasive characters go totally unblocked for the first 3 turns. Usually a blowout if aggro draws a normal hand.

**Game Plan (Emerald/Ruby):**
- Curve out perfectly: 1-drop, 2-drop, 3-drop, close by turn 6
- Quest with Evasive characters before ramp establishes any defensive presence
- If opponent plays a big threat, use Ruby removal to answer it and keep questing
- This matchup is about raw speed — don't get cute with combat tricks

**If Playing Ramp vs. This Matchup:**
- You need to draw Bodyguard characters and get them down by turn 3
- Amber healing buys you 1–2 extra turns; use them to jam a big threat
- Honestly, if aggro curves out cleanly, you lose. Focus on bricking their draws.

## The Mirror Match Principles

### Ruby/Sapphire Mirror
- **Who has more cards wins.** Sapphire's draw effects are the primary edge.
- Board presence matters less than lore total until one player is 3+ ahead.
- Save Dragon Fire for the opponent's most efficient questers (highest lore-per-ink).
- Don't trade into unfavorable challenges — lore racing is usually correct.

### Amethyst/Steel Mirror
- **Bounce wars.** Whoever bounces more efficiently wins.
- A Whole New World timing is critical — play it when you have more cards than opponent.
- Ward characters are extremely strong in this mirror; protect them at all costs.
- The first player to land a Ward + big body and stabilize usually wins.

### Emerald/Ruby Aggro Mirror
- **The fastest hand wins.** Very little to outplay here.
- Evasive vs. Evasive — removal becomes more important as the only answer.
- Fire the Cannons! is the best mirror card; clear their 1-drops efficiently.
- Keep at least 1 removal spell for the single biggest Evasive threat.

## Mulliganing for Matchups

When you know the matchup going into game 2/3:

**vs. Aggro:**
- Keep any hand with 1–2 Bodyguard characters OR 2+ cheap removal spells
- Mulligan hands with no turn-2 or turn-3 play

**vs. Control:**
- Keep hands with 2–3 threats at varying costs + 1 draw spell
- Mulligan hands that are all removal (they don't have much to remove)

**vs. Ramp:**
- Keep aggressive hands; anything that curves to turn 5
- Don't keep slow control hands — race them before threats land

**vs. Mirror:**
- Keep the hand that maximizes your draw effects
- Both colors must be represented on the opening hand
