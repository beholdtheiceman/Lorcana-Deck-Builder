# Lorcana Meta Archetypes Reference
_Last updated: July 2026 (Sets 1–6 / Azurite Sea era)_

This file describes the current competitive archetypes the agent should know about. When a user asks about "the meta," "what's good," or "what am I facing," draw from this file.

---

## Tier 1 — Dominant

### Ruby/Sapphire Midrange
**Inks:** Ruby + Sapphire  
**Archetype:** Midrange  
**Game Plan:** The most consistent archetype in the game. Uses Ruby's early aggression and removal to stabilize the board while Sapphire's card draw and ramp ensures it never runs out of gas. Wins the lore race through consistent tempo — threatening to quest every turn while being able to challenge when needed.

**Core Strategy:**
- Flood the board with efficient 2–4 cost characters
- Use Sapphire draw (Let It Go, Friends on the Other Side) to maintain hand advantage
- Transition to powerful 5–6 drop threats mid-game
- Win by staying 2–3 lore ahead and forcing bad challenges from the opponent

**Win Conditions:** Outpacing on lore while controlling key threats; closing out with large Sapphire or Ruby characters

**Key Cards:**
- Moana - Of Motunui (Sapphire, low-cost, high-lore threat)
- Maui - Hero to All (versatile, bounce protection)
- Elsa - Snow Queen (card draw + board presence)
- Fire the Cannons! (cheap Ruby removal)
- Let It Go (Sapphire song, mass card draw)
- Dragon Fire (Ruby removal)
- Stitch - Carefree Surfer (efficient early threat)
- Belle - Strange but Special (reliable mid-curve)

**Weaknesses:** Struggles against heavy bounce (Amethyst), can be raced by pure aggro if it stumbles on ink, loses to Ward characters it can't remove

### Amethyst/Steel Control
**Inks:** Amethyst + Steel  
**Archetype:** Control / Big Threats  
**Game Plan:** Uses Amethyst's bounce effects to reset the opponent's board while Steel's large characters and items provide inevitability. Plays from behind comfortably. Wins by out-valuing in the late game.

**Core Strategy:**
- Bounce opponent's best characters with Be Prepared, Return to the Sea, etc.
- Set up Steel's large Bodyguard / Resist characters as sticky threats
- Draw cards aggressively with Amethyst effects
- Win by overwhelming the opponent with card advantage and unanswerable threats

**Win Conditions:** Resolving an unanswerable threat (Yen Sid - Powerful Sorcerer, Maleficent - Monstrous Dragon) behind protection, or simply bouncing everything until opponent decks out of resources

**Key Cards:**
- Yen Sid - Powerful Sorcerer (massive Steel threat)
- Maleficent - Monstrous Dragon (board wipe + lore)
- Be Prepared (Amethyst song, board wipe)
- Return to the Sea (bounce + tempo)
- Genie - On the Job (Amethyst draw engine)
- The Beast - Hardheaded (Steel Ward threat)
- A Whole New World (mass card draw)
- Pawpsicle (item-based card draw)

**Weaknesses:** Slow start; loses badly to decks that flood the board faster than bounce can keep up; poor ink consistency if both colors aren't drawn early

### Emerald/Ruby Aggro
**Inks:** Emerald + Ruby  
**Archetype:** Aggro  
**Game Plan:** The fastest deck in the format. Floods the board with Evasive characters that opponents can't easily challenge and Reckless / Rush characters to remove blockers. Aims to reach 20 lore by turn 6–7.

**Core Strategy:**
- Curve out with 1–3 cost Evasive characters
- Use Ruby removal to clear anything blocking the lore race
- Emerald disruption (bounce, ping effects) to slow opponent
- Race to 20 lore before the opponent can stabilize

**Win Conditions:** Pure lore race; opponent can't challenge Evasive characters and falls behind

**Key Cards:**
- Simba - Protective Cub (1-cost Evasive, early lore)
- Tinker Bell - Giant Fairy (Evasive, efficient cost)
- Peter Pan - Never Landing (Evasive lore machine)
- Fire the Cannons! (cheap removal to clear blockers)
- Grab Your Sword (combat trick / removal)
- Wendy Darling - Escapee (Evasive value)
- Dinglehopper (item-based draw)

**Weaknesses:** Ward characters shut off key removal; falls apart if behind on board by turn 4; poor late game if the race stalls

## Tier 2 — Competitive

### Amber/Sapphire Ramp
**Inks:** Amber + Sapphire  
**Archetype:** Ramp / Big Threats  
**Game Plan:** Uses Amber's Support keyword (characters that buff questing teammates) and Sapphire's ramp effects to accelerate into enormous 7+ cost characters ahead of schedule. A slower, more combo-oriented strategy that wins when it gets big threats down early.

**Core Strategy:**
- Use Support characters to generate bonus lore while ramping
- Sapphire's "look at top X, put cards in inkwell" effects to hit land drops early
- Threaten massive characters by turn 5–6 instead of turn 7–8

**Win Conditions:** An unanswerable 8–9 cost character questing repeatedly before opponent can respond

**Key Cards:**
- Fishbone Quill (Sapphire ramp item)
- Cogsworth - Grandfather Clock (Amber support)
- Goofy - Super Goof (Sapphire ramp)
- Hiram Flaversham - Toymaker (Amber Support)
- Tamatoa - So Shiny! (massive lore threat)
- Madam Mim - Snake (ramp payoff)

**Weaknesses:** Vulnerable to early aggression before ramp comes online; bounce resets entire game plan; struggles if key ramp pieces are removed

---

### Ruby/Amber Aggressive Midrange
**Inks:** Ruby + Amber  
**Archetype:** Aggressive Midrange  
**Game Plan:** Combines Ruby's aggression with Amber's healing and Support keyword to out-sustain opponents in the mid-game lore race. Not as fast as pure aggro but more resilient.

**Core Strategy:**
- Play efficient early Ruby threats
- Use Amber's healing (Pascal, healing effects) to offset any damage taken
- Support characters multiply lore output of questing characters
- Outlast opponent through sustained lore advantage

**Win Conditions:** Establishing an uncontested board of Support + questing characters that generate 4–6 lore per turn

**Key Cards:**
- Rapunzel - Gifted with Healing (healing + value)
- Pascal - Rapunzel's Companion (Support)
- Flynn Rider - Frying Pan (efficient Ruby threat)
- Merida - Brave Bowman (Ruby challenger)
- Enchanted Rose (Amber item, healing + card draw)

**Weaknesses:** Slow against pure aggro; Support characters are often low-willpower and die to pings; no great comeback mechanic when behind

---

### Sapphire/Steel Big Stuff
**Inks:** Sapphire + Steel  
**Archetype:** Ramp / Control  
**Game Plan:** Steel's defensive characters (Bodyguard, Resist) buy time while Sapphire ramps into enormous Steel threats. A very slow but powerful late-game strategy.

**Core Strategy:**
- Deploy Steel Bodyguard characters to absorb challenges
- Ramp aggressively with Sapphire
- Win by landing unanswerable Steel threats (high Resist, high Willpower)

**Win Conditions:** A Steel character with Resist/Ward that generates lore while being nearly unkillable

**Key Cards:**
- Hades - King of Olympus (Steel massive threat)
- Kronk - Pacha's Friend (Steel Bodyguard value)
- Merlin - Rabbit (Sapphire ramp)
- Fishbone Quill (ramp item)
- The Prince - Never Giving Up (Ward + Bodyguard)

**Weaknesses:** Very slow to close; bounce completely resets it; poor in aggro-heavy metas

## Tier 3 — Rogue / Fringe

### Amber/Amethyst Midrange
Healing + Shift value deck. Strong in theory but outclassed by Tier 1 in most matchups. Playable in casual.

### Emerald/Sapphire Control
Bounce + draw engine. Fragile but powerful when it curves out. Sees play as a meta counter.

### Steel/Ruby Aggro-Control
Hybrid aggro with Resist threats. Uncommon but punishes slow decks hard.
