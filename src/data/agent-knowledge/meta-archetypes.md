# Meta Archetypes — Set 12 (Wilds Unknown) Competitive Landscape
_Last updated from Metafy competitive guides — Set 12 (Wilds Unknown). This represents the current Core Constructed meta._

---

## Meta Overview

Set 12 (Wilds Unknown) has established a clear power hierarchy. The top tier is dominated by three oppressively powerful strategies. The decks below them are competitive but must be "better" — better sequencing, better reads — to overcome the raw power gap.

**Key Set 12 Context:**
- Most top decks are heavily rotation-proof with set 9+ composition
- "Burn lore" effects (Giant Cobra, Gathering Knowledge and Wisdom, Dash Parr) are a new threat vector
- Item removal is a key tech consideration — Enigmatic Inkcaster and Snowfort go unchecked by many decks
- Ruby toys (Hand in the Box, Wind-Up Frog, etc.) can be played for free, enabling explosive board floods

---

## Tier 1 — Tournament Dominant

### Dogs (Amber/Emerald — multiple variants)
**Game Plan:** Aggressive singer deck. Deploy low-cost characters who can sing Under the Sea to generate card advantage and lore. Two main variants: pure Dogs and Toys variant.

**Core Strategy:**
- Curve out with singers early (turns 1–3)
- Use Under the Sea to disrupt opponent's board while gaining card advantage
- Win via lore accumulation before opponent can stabilize

**Key Cards:**
- Under the Sea (key song — sung by low-cost dog characters)
- Chromicon (Emerald — hurts "when played" abilities; opponent must sequence around it)

**Win Conditions:** Lore race via consistent card advantage engine. Chromicon slows reactive decks. Opponent either runs out of answers or can't keep pace with lore generation.

**Weaknesses:**
- Characters must challenge or quest to win — vulnerable to efficient removal
- Toys variant: Sid Phillips hits YOUR toys too in the mirror

**Role vs. Common Opponents:** Always Beatdown. Most aggressive Tier 1 deck.

---

### Blurple (Amethyst/Sapphire)
**Game Plan:** High-interaction control deck using targeted removal and bounce to dismantle opponent's board, then win via superior card quality.

**Core Strategy:**
- Play at most 1–2 things per turn while removing 1–2 opponent threats
- Accumulate board advantage through efficient challenge trades
- Demona as a board swing when opponent has multiple challengers

**Key Cards:**
- Merryweather (4/5 Ward — primary blocker/challenger; hard to remove)
- Elsa (shifted — strong body, synergy with Demona)
- Demona (board swing — banishes opponent's challengers)
- Hades (resource generation)
- Tipo (efficient early challenger)
- Dumbo (Evasive threat)
- Cheshire Cat (damage redirection/disruption)

**Win Conditions:** Grind opponent out of resources via Demona swings. Merryweather → Elsa → Demona → Hades is the "nut draw."

**Weaknesses:**
- Only 1–2 plays per turn — vulnerable to wide board flooding
- No board wipe (outside of Demona + challengers)
- If Demona can't banish anything (opponent has no challengers), her impact collapses
- Struggles vs. decks that dump their hand before Demona turns

**Countering Blurple:**
- Force them into challenges early — sacrifice a character to reduce Demona's targets
- Dump your hand BEFORE their Demona turn so you can rebuild
- Bodies win this matchup — let their removal ramp you; skip inking low-cost characters, just play them
- Babyhead is crucial here for card flow; Candy Drift answers Merryweather

---

### Amber/Steel
**Game Plan:** Ramp-enabled threat deck. Akood enables playing top-end characters 2 turns early. Strength of a Raging Fire is the key song for massive lore swings.

**Core Strategy:**
- Early Akood enables 5–7 cost characters by turns 3–4
- Strength of a Raging Fire buffs characters for massive challenges/quests
- Angel provides tempo — punishes resource-heavy opponents

**Key Cards:**
- Akood (ramp enabler — allows playing high-cost cards early)
- Strength of a Raging Fire (key song — buffs character strength significantly)
- Angel (tempo — recurring damage; opponent needs to maintain low hand size)

**Win Conditions:** Overpower opponent with early large threats. Strength of a Raging Fire creates lethal challenge situations.

**Weaknesses:**
- Vulnerable to 2-willpower characters (dodge Angel's recurring damage)
- Disrupting their singers reduces Strength of a Raging Fire impact
- Going longer gives opponent time to match their board
- Toys variant: Sid hurts this variant in the mirror

**Countering Amber Steel:**
- Maintain 2-willpower characters on board
- Disrupt their singers (reduces song value)
- Candy Drift removes their large characters when they make greedy plays

## Tier 2 — Competitive

### Amber/Ruby Toys
**Game Plan:** Character-based midrange. Flood the board with free/discounted Ruby toys, use card draw engines (Jesse, Babyhead, Woody), and pivot between Disruption and Pressure paths in the mid-game.

**Two Mid-Game Paths:**
1. **Disruption Path:** Headless Horseman + Sid Phillips control the board while Jesse/Babyhead maintain card advantage
2. **Pressure Path:** Wide board forces opponent to split answers; quest to 20 over 2 turns

**Core Cards (mostly rotation-proof from Set 12):**
- Woody (1-cost): Preferred 1-drop; 5-cost Shift gives all toys +1 willpower and quests to draw 1
- Bullseye (2-cost): Sings You've Got a Friend in Me; strong early challenger
- Hamm (2-cost): Backup 2-drop; enables turn-3 Horseman or Woody Shift
- Jesse Woodard: Card draw + shrinks opposing challengers (protects your board)
- Babyhead: Draw engine + power boost when you pay 2 or less (small toys trade up)
- Hand in the Box (free Ruby toy): Hold for explosive dump turns
- Wind-Up Frog (free Ruby toy): Hold for explosive dump turns
- Bouncing Ducky: Costs 1 less per toy in discard — often free; 5/5/2 body
- Headless Horseman (5-cost): Catch-all removal after Jesse trigger
- Sid Phillips (6-cost): Forces opponent to banish their own character (NOT just your toys — opponent's toys also trigger this in the mirror)
- You've Got a Friend in Me: Bullseye sings → top 4, add 2 toys → near-guaranteed +2 cards
- Be Our Guest (2-cost song): Finds Horseman/Sid; simulates strong play without Bullseye
- Candy Drift: Lets small toys trade into large characters solo

**Optimal Opening Lines:**
- Woody (1) → Bullseye (2) → Sing You've Got a Friend in Me + Shift Woody (5) → quest/free play
- Woody (1) → Hamm (2) → Babyhead or Woody (4)
- Woody (1) → any 2-drop → Be Our Guest + Shift Woody (5)

**Win Conditions:**
- Flood board; quest to 20 before opponent stabilizes
- Horseman + Horseman + Sid clears board and generates lore burst
- Reach 16+ lore, then Sid triggers finish the game

**Weaknesses:**
- No item removal (Enigmatic Inkcaster and Snowfort go unchecked)
- Burn lore effects bypass board state
- Multiple consecutive board wipes are hard to recover from
- Lower raw power than Tier 1 — must out-sequence to win

**Critical Sequencing Rules:**
1. ALWAYS draw first — Jesse, Woody, Be Our Guest, Friend in Me before ANY ink commitment
2. Quest Jesse before Woody (5) — Woody's free-play trigger uses the draw
3. Clear the board before playing Sid — fewer opponent characters = opponent has fewer "choice" options
4. Hold Hand and Frog for explosive post-Sid or post-trade dump turns
5. DO NOT miss lethal chasing additional value

### YP Dale (Amethyst/Amber — "Yellow Purple")
**Game Plan:** Dale enables powerful follow-up plays. Supported by Amethyst package (card draw, bounce, efficient challenges). Strong post-Wilds Unknown.

**Key Cards:**
- Dale (core enabler — buffs all follow-up characters)
- Elsa (shifted — 5-strength challenger with Dale on board)
- Genie (resource generation)
- Cheshire Cat (damage redirection/disruption)

**Win Conditions:** Dale + Elsa generates insurmountable value via challenges. Deck snowballs once Dale sticks.

**Weaknesses:**
- Significantly weaker without Dale on board
- Jesse triggers don't fire with Dale on board (reduces opponent's engine)
- Multiple Headless Horseman answers keep Dale off the table

**Removal Priority when facing YP Dale:** Dale > Genie/Elsa/Cat (board-state dependent)

---

### Sapphire/Steel
**Game Plan:** Late-game inevitability. Merida and Three Arrows. Goliath is the card-draw engine that "unlocks" the deck. Lacks mass removal.

**Key Cards:**
- Goliath (both players draw 2 each turn — deck built to exploit this more than opponent)
- Merida / Three Arrows (threats and removal)
- Tinker Bell (dangerous challenger)

**Win Conditions:** Out-resource opponent in the late game via Goliath card draw. High-quality threats become unanswerable once unlocked.

**Weaknesses:**
- Dumps hand to reach Goliath — if Goliath is removed, falls to drawing 1/turn
- No mass removal
- Fast decks close before they unlock

**Countering Sapphire Steel:** Kill Goliath. Do NOT use your own draw effects when they have Goliath — it benefits them more. Race to 20.

---

### Amethyst/Steel (multiple variants)
**Game Plan:** Combat-focused control. Characters challenge up efficiently or gain lore while challenging. Dwarves variant (less interaction) and traditional variant.

**Key Cards:**
- Yzma (card advantage engine — must be removed immediately)
- Cheshire Cat (disruption)

**Win Conditions:** Win board through superior challenge trades, quest to 20.

**Weaknesses:** Wide boards spread decision points. Yzma removal neutralizes their advantage.

---

### Ruby/Amethyst
**Game Plan:** Go-wide pressure with Jesse, Della, and character synergies. Elisa Meza shuts off opponent's triggered abilities.

**Key Cards:**
- Elisa Meza (shuts off Jesse/Della triggers — powerful defensive/offensive piece)
- Jesse / Della (synergy package)

**Win Conditions:** Outpace via character synergies and efficient combat.

**Weaknesses:** No board wipe. Force them to challenge to remove Elisa, which re-enables your synergies.

---

### Emerald/Sapphire Control
**Game Plan:** Late-game domination. Mass removal, Prince Phillip combo, Mass Magical Scouring as key board wipe. Slowest setup of all meta decks.

**Key Cards:**
- Prince Phillip (shifted — Evasive/high-lore threat, the combo enabler)
- Mass Magical Scouring (MMS) — board wipe
- Draw/bounce package

**Win Conditions:** Survive, board wipe multiple times, resolve Prince Phillip combo.

**Weaknesses:**
- Slowest to set up — worst matchup vs. early aggression
- MMS used on small threats wastes it for Prince Phillip follow-up
- If opponent reaches 16+ lore, Sid Phillips can steal the game after the board wipe

## Format Notes

- **Core Constructed:** Primary tournament format. Primarily Sets 9–12 (Wilds Unknown)
- **Rotation:** Most Set 12 cards are rotation-proof — expect these archetypes to persist into future sets
- **Meta shifts with each new set** — always verify current tier list against recent tournament results
- **Burn lore** (Giant Cobra, Gathering Knowledge and Wisdom, Dash Parr) is an emerging threat vector that bypasses board state
