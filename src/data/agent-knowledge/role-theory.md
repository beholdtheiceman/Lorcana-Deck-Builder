# Role Theory — Who Is the Beatdown?
_The most important strategic concept in Lorcana. Read this before giving any gameplay advice._

---

## The Core Concept

Every Lorcana game has two roles: **the Beatdown** and **the Control**.

- **The Beatdown** is the deck applying pressure. It races to 20 lore. Every turn it isn't advancing its game plan is a turn wasted. It trades resources to stay ahead.
- **The Control** is the deck reacting. It disrupts the beatdown's plan, stabilizes, and wins in the late game with superior card quality or inevitability.

**The critical insight:** These roles are NOT fixed to archetypes. They are determined by the matchup. A deck that is the beatdown against one opponent becomes the control against another.

Getting your role wrong is the most common high-level mistake in Lorcana. A control player trying to race a faster deck loses. A beatdown player trying to grind a slower deck loses.

## Role Assignments by Matchup

### Ruby/Sapphire Midrange

| Opponent | Ruby/Sap Role | Why |
|----------|--------------|-----|
| vs. Amethyst/Steel Control | **Beatdown** | Ruby/Sap is faster; Amethyst/Steel wins if the game goes long. Apply pressure constantly. |
| vs. Emerald/Ruby Aggro | **Control** | Emerald/Ruby is faster; Ruby/Sap must stabilize and grind. Don't race — survive and out-resource. |
| vs. Amber/Sapphire Ramp | **Beatdown** | Ramp wins if it resolves big threats. Ruby/Sap must close before that happens. |
| vs. Sapphire/Steel Big Stuff | **Beatdown** | Same logic as vs. ramp — close before the late game arrives. |
| vs. Ruby/Sapphire (mirror) | **Tempo** | Neither is purely beatdown. Whoever generates more card advantage wins. |

### Amethyst/Steel Control

| Opponent | Ameth/Steel Role | Why |
|----------|-----------------|-----|
| vs. Ruby/Sapphire Midrange | **Control** | Ruby/Sap applies tempo pressure; Ameth/Steel must survive and out-value. |
| vs. Emerald/Ruby Aggro | **Control** | Hardest matchup; Ameth/Steel is always reacting. Must survive to Be Prepared range. |
| vs. Amber/Sapphire Ramp | **Beatdown** | Rare case: Ameth/Steel is the faster threat. Apply bounce pressure before ramp stabilizes. |
| vs. Sapphire/Steel Big Stuff | **Beatdown** | Both are slow, but Ameth/Steel's bounce disrupts Steel ramp faster than Steel can answer Ameth threats. |

### Emerald/Ruby Aggro

| Opponent | Em/Ruby Role | Why |
|----------|-------------|-----|
| vs. Ruby/Sapphire Midrange | **Beatdown** | Always racing. Every Evasive character that quests is a win. |
| vs. Amethyst/Steel Control | **Beatdown** | Go wide and fast before Be Prepared. This is the best matchup. |
| vs. Amber/Sapphire Ramp | **Beatdown** | Close before ramp resolves anything threatening. |
| vs. Emerald/Ruby (mirror) | **Tempo** | Both are beatdown. The player who answers the opponent's Evasive threats while advancing their own wins. |

### Amber/Sapphire Ramp

| Opponent | Amb/Sap Role | Why |
|----------|-------------|-----|
| vs. Any aggro | **Control** | Must survive to the late game. Bodyguards, healing, and stall are the plan. |
| vs. Amethyst/Steel | **Beatdown** | Rare: ramp's big threats can outpace Ameth/Steel's bounce if landed early enough. |
| vs. Ruby/Sapphire | **Control** | Can't out-tempo Ruby/Sap early; must survive and threaten with large characters. |

## How Role Determines Decision-Making

Once you know your role, your entire priority system changes.

### If You Are the Beatdown:

1. **Quest every turn possible.** Every turn you don't quest, you fall behind your game plan.
2. **Only challenge to remove something that directly blocks your lore race** — a character with high lore, a Bodyguard protecting questers, or an Evasive character you can actually answer.
3. **Do not trade resources evenly.** Even trades favor the control deck. You need asymmetric trades (spend 1 removal spell to clear their 4-cost threat = good; spend your 4-cost character to kill their 2-cost = bad).
4. **Play around the control deck's specific answer** — if they're Amethyst, don't overextend into Be Prepared. If they're Steel, don't race their Bodyguard characters, go around them.
5. **Inking is aggressive.** Ink high-cost cards you don't need right now to hit your low-cost curve plays.

### If You Are the Control:

1. **Do not race.** Questing when behind on board hands the game to the beatdown. Prioritize board stability first.
2. **Challenge to protect your board** — remove their best questers so your own characters can survive to quest later.
3. **Value every card.** Don't spend removal on small threats unless they're actively threatening your position. Save answers for key threats.
4. **Play for the late game.** If the game reaches turn 8+, you should be favored. Don't take risky lines that could let them close early.
5. **Inking is patient.** Ink cards you won't need in this matchup to ensure you hit your high-cost threats.
6. **Heal and protect.** If your deck has healing (Amber) or Bodyguard characters, deploy them before your questers.

### If You Are in a Tempo Matchup (mirror):

1. Card advantage is king — whoever has more cards in hand after turn 5 wins.
2. Draw spells take priority over development.
3. Efficient challenges (kill their best threat with your worst character) are more valuable than in any other role.
4. Protect your draw engine over your lore engine.

## Role Fluidity — When Roles Shift Mid-Game

Roles can shift during a game based on the board state. Always re-evaluate your role when:

**The beatdown should switch to control when:**
- You're up 8+ lore with a stable board — now you're protecting a lead, not racing
- Your opponent's board is completely empty — stop challenging, start questing everything
- You've run low on cards — switch to grinding mode; don't race on empty

**The control should switch to beatdown when:**
- Opponent is at 15+ lore — you MUST apply pressure now or lose
- Opponent has an unanswerable threat that will win in 2 turns — race them before it can activate
- Your bounce/removal has depleted — you can't out-grind; shift to applying lore pressure

## Common Role Mistakes to Flag in Game Reviews

When reviewing gameplay, flag these as role errors:

**Beatdown mistakes:**
- Challenging a non-threatening character instead of questing (wasted tempo)
- Saving removal for "something better" while opponent quests freely (control thinking while playing beatdown)
- Overextending into a board wipe when a slightly smaller board would have been fine
- Inking low-cost early plays when you need them to stay on curve (saving ink for "value" that never comes)

**Control mistakes:**
- Questing with all characters when behind on board (race thinking while playing control)
- Using removal on small threats when the opponent's key threat is still active
- Not blocking/challenging to protect your lore lead when it's established
- Treating every game like it's a race when your deck wins the long game

**Role confusion mistakes:**
- Playing a midrange deck as pure control when the opponent is slower (should be beatdown)
- Playing a midrange deck as pure aggro when opponent is faster (should be control)
- Not adjusting role after a key card is bounced or removed (game state changed; reassess)
